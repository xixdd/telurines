## 90% of the content is free.

Yes, that's true, all filters and tools are available completely for free. However, there are things that you can't access.

The most important of those being cloud storage and customized brushes.

First of all you need and account, for foreigners the easiest way, and actually the only one possible for 99% of us, is WeChat, and still it's not something everyone can have as you might know the verification requires often a friend who can scan your QR code.<br>
Now, this is not about WeChat so i won't explain how, but after managing to have an account, i connected it to 画世界Pro, and let me tell you that you're not missing out on anything.<br>

Even after creating an account you can't even do the smallest things such as changing you HuaShijie profile picture... Now that's not very important but it tells you what to expect.<br>

First, the cloud storage is poor, so bad I won't even mention it because it's so ridiculous. And if you want to have more, you have to pay.<br>
Of course it is completely worth it, this app is one of the best you'll find, but most of you might not want to pay —nor i did.

## Premium 

To access all that, you need, as i said before, premium.
And again as i said, i believe it's worth it. 

You can already animate with a customized resolution, unlike ibis paint, that's for free, and it's actually pretty good, however, it getsbr> better.< If you have premium, you can directly add and ajust sounds in you animation... No other app is this complete, either you have good animation tools but poor brush system, or the opposite, this is huge for those who want to animate so you may consider going premium.

This also unlocks pdf exports, brush creation, community assets and more.

## Size matters

Because something that small can't possibly satisfy me.

### 5000px x 500px canvases

That is roughly your limit, it's because it's been optimized for mobile devices that you are limited, this ensures there's no lag.
You can try to expand it as much as possible but you won't get anything very much better, this is in the design, it doesn't depend on your phone.

And the bigger your canvas is, the fewer your layers can be. Yes, ther are limits on how many you can have.

### It doesn't stop there

Brushes are also limited in size. For me this was a huge disappointment. If this wasn't like that, 画世界Pro would be the #1 app in the world, but nothing can't be that good, not even chinese stuff.

If you're coming from ibis paint, where the max size of a brush is over 16,000 pixels, this will affect you seriously, it did for me, there are workarounds but it's not the same...
