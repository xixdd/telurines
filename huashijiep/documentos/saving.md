# Saving Progress and Exporting

___
___
## /!\ Worth noting: by 'export' here I mean saving to phone. Just in case anyone was confused.
___
___

Alright, now I'll explain exactly what the title says.
To start, it's worth mentioning that Hua Shijie has an auto-save system, though <mark>it's always best to save your progress manually</mark> every now and then. This is even more important if you're going to leave the app in the background for a long time, or if your device isn't very powerful.

What you need to do is as simple as opening the settings wheel. The very first icon you'll see is the save progress one.

![](images/imgsave/1.png)

In case you're wondering, the second one — which looks the same but with a pencil — is used to make a copy of the project and give it a different name.

___
___


## Export

The process is quite similar. Open the same menu, just switch to the <mark>square icon with a diagonal arrow</mark> at the top.

![](images/imgsave/2.png)

There you can choose from different export formats.

___

To export as a transparent/no-background PNG, you need to open the layers menu and hide the background layer.
The "background layer" is easy to identify because, besides being at the very bottom, it has a slightly darker shade.

![](images/imgsave/3.png)

Hide the background and export as PNG.

___

Whenever you export anything, this menu will appear.
The top row won't be useful since it's for sharing with Chinese apps like QQ, Weixin, etc. So you'll use the bottom row, which only has 2 options: a photo and a folder.

![](images/imgsave/4.png)


The folder saves it to your default pictures or camera folder on your phone.

The photo option saves it to a new folder that is automatically created called "画世界Pro".

Pick whichever you prefer.
